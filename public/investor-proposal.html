Section 03
Our Solution
Professional invoicing at half the price — with a payment link built into every invoice.

SlateInvoice is a clean, fast, and affordable invoicing platform built specifically for freelancers and small businesses. It does what FreshBooks does — for $7/month instead of $20/month — with one feature no competitor has nailed: a public payment page on every invoice.

🔗
The Pay Invoice Online Feature
Every invoice created in SlateInvoice gets a unique public URL at slateinvoice.com/pay/[id]. Share it via email, text, or WhatsApp. Your client clicks the link, sees the invoice, and pays by card — no login, no account, no friction. The invoice automatically marks itself as paid via Stripe webhook. This is the last mile of getting paid, solved.

Core Philosophy

⚡ Fast

Create and send a professional invoice in under 2 minutes. No onboarding, no tutorials, no setup wizard.

💰 Affordable

Starter at $7/mo. Freelancer at $15/mo. Business at $25/mo. Up to 75% cheaper than FreshBooks for equivalent features.

🎯 Focused

No payroll module, no inventory, no audit trail. Just invoicing, client management, and getting paid — done well.

📱 Modern

Built on Next.js 14, Railway, PostgreSQL, Stripe. Scalable, fast, and ready for mobile.

Total Addressable Market (TAM)

The global invoicing software market is valued at $4.5B+ and is projected to reach $7.5B by 2030. The US alone accounts for roughly 40% of this market. With 59 million freelancers and 33 million small businesses, the demand for simple, affordable invoicing is structural — not cyclical.

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)

Our target: US-based solo freelancers and micro-businesses (1-10 employees) who currently use spreadsheets, Word documents, PayPal, or free tools — and are ready to upgrade for under $20/month. Conservative estimate: 8 million potential users in the US alone.

Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)

A realistic 24-month target of 5,000 paying subscribers represents 0.06% of the SAM. At $14 average ARPU, that's $70,000 MRR ($840K ARR) — achievable with focused paid acquisition and organic SEO.

Why This Market, Why Now

📈 Freelance economy is structural

Remote work normalization, rise of independent contractors, and portfolio careers are permanent trends. The IRS estimates 30%+ of US workers will be fully self-employed by 2030.

💸 Incumbents are overpriced

FreshBooks raised prices 3x in 5 years. QuickBooks now starts at $35/mo. The market is creating a price gap at the low end that SlateInvoice fills precisely.

🔍 SEO opportunity is open

Keywords like "free invoice generator" get 500K+ monthly searches. The top results are outdated tools with poor UX. A modern, well-designed product ranks.

💳 Online payment adoption

Post-pandemic, clients expect to pay online. "Send me a check" is becoming unacceptable. The payment link feature directly addresses this shift.

Go-to-Market Strategy
Three channels: paid search, organic SEO, and word-of-mouth flywheel.

Channel 1: Google Ads (Immediate)

Target keywords: "invoice software," "best invoicing app freelancers," "FreshBooks alternative," "free invoice generator," "send invoice online." Estimated CPC: $2–$5. Conversion rate target: 3–5%. Conversion tracking already installed. Budget: $5,000/mo post-funding.

Channel 2: SEO Content (Compounding)

15+ blog posts already published targeting high-volume, bottom-of-funnel queries. "How to write a professional invoice" alone gets 40K+ monthly searches. As posts rank, organic CAC approaches $0 and compounds over time. Target: 30% of new signups from organic within 12 months.

Channel 3: Word-of-Mouth & Referrals

Every invoice sent from SlateInvoice includes "Powered by SlateInvoice" in the footer (removable on Business plan). The Pay Invoice Online page is seen by every client who pays an invoice — each one is a potential new customer. Referral program planned for Q3 2026.

Channel 4: Vertical Communities

Target: freelancer communities on Reddit (r/freelance, r/forhire), Facebook Groups, Discord servers. Authentic participation + free plan converts community members to users. Budget: time, not money.

Channel 5: White-Label Partnerships

Partner with accounting firms, co-working spaces, and professional associations to offer SlateInvoice as a branded tool for their members. Pipeline: ECMTuner community, automotive industry networks.
